Transport

The most popular way to get around Nairobi is by use of public transport systems. Available modes of transport include buses, trains, boda bodas (motorcycles) , taxis and tuk tuks. The use of matatus is currently the most widely used means to get around Nairobi.. They are easily recognizable by the yellow stripe painted on their sides. They usually stand because they are painted over with great designs and some offer additional services such as free wifi. Within Nairobi it will be rare to exceed 100shillings , ( 1 dollar) in a single commute. You have not experienced Nairobi until you have ridden in a matatu!

Money

You do not need to worry about carrying money with you. Majority of establishments will accept visa/mastercard. There are plenty of atms all around the city and areas with a high population density. There are also plenty of forex bureaus all around the country that have fair rates for all. There are also plenty of mobile solutions to help make it easy for you to pay for that which you desire.

Security

Being in a new place can be scary and with all the travel bans and warnings that Nairobi has received in the past it is only natural that many of you would be wary of moving around. We highly advise that you register with your embassy as soon as you arrive. If there is any major incident, the consulate will know you may need their help or can at least contact your loved ones back home. In case you find yourself in an emergency situation, the general emergency number is 999. If you are involved in an accident, call the police immediately and wait until they arrive. Additionally, make sure you also know the number of your nearest police station. Should you fall sick, there are plenty of hospitals and clinics around Nairobi, including flying doctor services to assist you.
