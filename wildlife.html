The Drive in the National Park

Located in the heart of Nairobi, Nairobi National Park is one of the only national parks on earth that borders a capital city. If you do not have much time to go to other national parks, this is worth a visit. You get to see most of the animals that you would on a regular safari. It is important to hire a driver who is familiar with the game park, routes and sites as he or she would know where the animals are. One can see many animals and get some excellent photos. Arriving early at about 6:00am is recommended. You will get to see the sunrise. There is also a higher probability of seeing many more animals. Animals reported to have been seen include, monkeys, giraffes, hippos, buffalo, rhinos, zebra's, antelopes, and many other animals including lions if you are lucky. Entrance fee is $ 50 and $ 25 for children It is located 14 km away from the Central Business District which is around 30 minutes depending on traffic For more information: Click here

The Daphne Sheldrick Elephant Sanctuary

The Elephant Orphanage is also located within the city of Nairobi. Not too far from the Nairobi national park. It is host to some of the largest mammals on earth and learn about their lives and how they survive. It is a great place to come and see these beautiful animals up close They only allow visitors between between 11am and 12pm, this is when feeding happens Entrance is $7 It is located 18 km away from the Central Business District which is around 40 minutes depending on traffic For more information Click here

Giraffe Center

Great place to begin your acquaintance with Giraffes and learn about them and other animals The highlight of this center is to see Giraffe up close and personal. There are always several giraffes, including newborns, on the grounds. The highlight of the tour is to feed a giraffe with one of the pellets that the staff hand out. You can hold the pellet in your hand or in your lips (if you're daring). It is located 24 km away from the Central Business District which is around 45 minutes depending on traffic Entrance fee is 1000 shillings ($10) for adults 500 shillings ($5) for children For more information Click here

Snake Park

The Nairobi Snake Park was started in January, 1961 to meet a popular attraction and to provide a research facility on reptiles, breeding of snakes. Live snakes were exhibited on experimental basis at the entrance of the Museum in 1958 which later became a popular attraction.When the popularity was noted, a portion of land in front of the Museum and down to the Nairobi River was acquired by the Museum Trustees for the development of Botanical gardens and exhibitions on live snakes. Currently the Snake Park exhibits major groups, Invertebrates like Giant Snails, Baboon Spider ,Mombasa Train Millipede, Crayfish, Freshwater Prawns and Vertebrates like Fishes both Marine and Fresh water, Amphibians, Reptiles, Birds and Mammals. It is also offering services such as rescue and rehabilitation centre for reptiles (abandoned, confiscated, illegal collection), dissemination of information on aquarium fishes and reptiles as well as specialized talks on the same.
